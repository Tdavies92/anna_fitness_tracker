Main Workout · 3 Rounds · ~20 minutes
1
Glute Bridge
3 sets · 15 reps · 30s rest · Optional: DB on hips
▾
3
Sets
15
Reps
30s
Rest
  • Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart
  • Press your feet into the floor and drive your hips up to the ceiling
  • Squeeze your glutes hard at the top — hold for 1–2 seconds
  • Lower slowly with control — don't let your hips crash down
  • Keep your core gently braced throughout — breathe out as you lift
  • To add resistance: place a dumbbell or kettlebell across your hips
💗 One of the safest and most effective post-partum exercises. Rebuilds glute strength and gently activates the pelvic floor. Exhale on the lift to engage deep core.
2
Goblet Squat
3 sets · 12 reps · 30s rest · KB or DB
▾
3
Sets
12
Reps
30s
Rest
  • Hold a KB or DB at your chest with both hands, elbows pointing down
  • Feet shoulder-width apart, toes turned out slightly
  • Sit back and down like lowering onto a chair — chest stays tall
  • Push your knees out in line with your toes as you descend
  • Drive through your heels to stand back up
  • Don't have a weight? Use bodyweight — still very effective
💗 Holding the weight at the chest helps keep your torso upright, reducing pressure on the pelvic floor compared to a back squat. Start light and build gradually.
3
Resistance Band Row
3 sets · 12 reps · 30s rest · Band anchored to door
▾
3
Sets
12
Reps
30s
Rest
  • Anchor your band at chest height in a door (or around a sturdy post)
  • Stand or sit facing the anchor point, holding both ends of the band
  • Pull both hands toward your lower ribs, driving elbows back
  • Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end of the pull
  • Slowly extend arms back to start — control the band, don't let it snap
  • Keep your back straight — don't round or lean back excessively
💗 Upper back strength is vital for new mums — hours of feeding and carrying can cause rounded shoulders and neck pain. This directly counteracts that.

3 sets · 10 reps · 30s rest
▾
3
Sets
10
Reps
30s
Rest
  • Sit on a chair or the floor with a straight back — no leaning against a back rest
  • Hold a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height, palms facing forward
  • Press both arms overhead until almost fully extended — don't lock out hard

💗 Seated is intentional — it reduces intra-abdominal pressure compared to standing, making it safer while your core is still rebuilding.

Dead Bug
3 sets · 8 reps each side · 30s rest · Bodyweight
▾
3
Sets
8
Each side
30s
Rest
  • Lie on your back with arms pointing straight up toward the ceiling
  • Lift both legs so knees are above hips, bent at 90 degrees (tabletop position)
  • Slowly lower your right arm back toward the floor while extending your left leg
  • Stop before either touches the floor — keep your lower back pressed down
  • Return to start, then repeat on the opposite side (left arm, right leg)
  • Exhale fully as you lower — this is the key to activating your deep core
💗 This is one of the best exercises for post-partum core rehabilitation. It works the deep stabilising muscles without putting pressure on a healing abdominal wall. Go slowly.

3 sets · 15 reps each side · 20s rest · Band optional
▾
3

  • Lie on your side with hips stacked, knees bent to about 45 degrees
  • Keep your feet together throughout the movement

💗 Hip abductor weakness is extremely common post-partum. Strong hip muscles protect your knees, back and pelvic floor. Don't skip these!

Main Workout · Circuit · 3 Rounds · ~20 minutes
1
KB / DB Romanian Deadlift
3 sets · 12 reps · 30s rest
▾
3
Sets
12
Reps
30s
Rest
  • Hold a KB or DB in each hand in front of your thighs, palms facing you
  • Soft bend in the knees — this is a hip hinge, not a squat
  • Push your hips back and let the weights slide down your legs
  • Go until you feel a strong stretch in your hamstrings — usually mid-shin
  • Drive hips forward and squeeze glutes to stand back up
  • Keep the weights close to your body the whole time — no swinging
💗 One of the best exercises for rebuilding your posterior chain (glutes and hamstrings) after pregnancy. Start with a light weight and focus on feeling the hamstrings work.
2
Reverse Lunge
3 sets · 10 reps each leg · 30s rest · Bodyweight or DBs
▾
3
Sets
10
Each leg
30s
Rest
  • Stand tall, feet hip-width apart
  • Step one foot back and lower your back knee toward the floor
  • Front knee stays above (not past) your front toes
  • Your front shin should be roughly vertical at the bottom
  • Push through your front heel to return to standing
  • Keep your chest upright — hands on hips or holding light DBs
💗 Reverse lunges are gentler on the knees than forward lunges and easier to control — ideal while rebuilding strength and balance after pregnancy.

Bent-Over Dumbbell Row
3 sets · 12 reps · 30s rest
▾
3
Sets
12
Reps
30s
Rest
  • Hold a DB in each hand, hinge forward from the hips about 45 degrees
  • Let the weights hang down in front of you, palms facing each other
  • Pull both elbows back and up toward your hips — squeeze shoulder blades
  • Lower back down with control — full arm extension
  • Keep your back flat throughout — no rounding
  • Breathe out as you pull, in as you lower
💗 Pairs perfectly with the chest press — one pulls, one pushes. Together they restore balanced upper body strength and help counteract the rounded posture from feeding.

Low-Impact Jumping Jacks
3 sets · 30 seconds · 20s rest
▾
3
Sets
30s
Duration
20s
Rest
  • Low-impact version: Step one foot out to the side while raising both arms overhead, then step back in — alternate sides
  • This gives you the cardio benefit without the impact of jumping
  • Move at a pace that raises your heart rate but you can still talk
  • If jumping feels fine and comfortable, regular jacks are also great

💗 High-impact movements like jumping can stress a post-partum pelvic floor. Always use the low-impact option until you've been cleared by a women's health physio for impact exercise.

Plank Hold
3 sets · 20–30 seconds · 20s rest · Knees or full
▾
3
Sets
20–30s
Hold
20s
Rest
  • Knee plank: Knees on the floor, arms straight, body in a straight line from knees to head
  • Full plank: Toes on the floor, arms straight, straight line from heels to head
  • Squeeze your core gently — imagine pulling your belly button toward your spine
  • Keep breathing — don't hold your breath!
  • Hips shouldn't sag down or poke up — find the straight line
  • Start with the knee version until you can hold 30s comfortably
💗 If you notice any doming or coning of your abdomen during a plank (a ridge running down the middle), drop to your knees or stop — this can indicate diastasis recti that needs specific attention.
